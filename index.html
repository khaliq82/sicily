Our Story

From Palermo to the
heart of Times Square

Sicily Osteria is an authentic Sicilian restaurant nestled in the heart of Times Square — built on a family story that began in Palermo and found its soul on 46th Street.

Robert Malta's journey to founding Sicily Osteria began with his mother, Dina — a seamstress who emigrated from Sicily to America on a work visa. As a single mother, she balanced multiple jobs to support her family, often bringing young Robert to her job at a bakery where he did his homework and learned early that dedication was not optional.

In 1974, Dina moved to work in a dress factory, picking Robert up from school before her second shift at the bakery. By 1982 she had returned to Sicily, but Robert stayed — committed to finishing high school in America. Through those years he washed dishes on weekends, and in college took a job as a server, quietly absorbing the rhythms of the restaurant world.

In 1988, Robert opened his first bar and restaurant. Two years later, Dina returned from Sicily to join him, and together they founded their first truly successful restaurant. That partnership — mother and son, Sicily and New York — became the foundation of everything Sicily Osteria stands for today.

Today, as President of NYCRG, Robert leads with the same tenacity his mother modelled: hard work, family first, and an absolute insistence on quality.

Where It All Comes From

Sicily is not just our name

Every ingredient on our table has a postcode in Sicily. From the volcanic slopes of Etna to the olive groves of Partanna — the island is present in every dish.
